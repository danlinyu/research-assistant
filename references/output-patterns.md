# Academic Output Patterns

## Idea Brief

Use after the initial interview.

```markdown
## Working Title
[tentative title]

## Problem Statement
[what problem the study addresses]

## Research Question
[primary question]

## Subquestions
- [subquestion]
- [subquestion]

## Candidate Contribution
[why this might add to the literature]

## Open Clarifications
- [what still needs to be answered]
```

## Literature Matrix

Use after the literature search.

```markdown
| Citation | Question | Data | Method | Main Finding | Limitation | Relevance | Access |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| [paper] | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | full text / abstract |
```

## Research Plan

Use after the literature review.

```markdown
## Final Question
[finalized question]

## Contribution
[gap and contribution]

## Data
- Publicly available now: [dataset + access path]
- Needed but not yet available: [what is missing]
- Acquisition plan: [how to get it]

## Analytical Schema
- Unit of analysis: [...]
- Variables: [...]
- Method: [...]
- Robustness checks: [...]
- Expected outputs: tables / figures / diagnostics

## Risks
- [risk]
- [risk]
```

## Journal Shortlist

Use once the study design is stable.

```markdown
| Journal | Why It Fits | Risks | Word Limit | Link |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| [journal] | ... | ... | ... | ... |
```

## IMRaD Draft Shell

Use when drafting has started but results may still be pending.

```markdown
## Introduction
[problem, literature, gap, contribution]

## Methods
[data, measures, design, analysis]

## Results
[actual results only, or explicit pending placeholders]

## Discussion
[interpretation, limitations, implications]

## Conclusion
[tight close]
```
