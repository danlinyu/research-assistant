# Research Plan

## Required Components

Turn the idea and literature review into an executable plan.

## Core Structure

### 1. Research Question And Contribution

- primary question
- secondary questions
- literature gap
- expected contribution

### 2. Design Choice

Specify the recommended design and the alternatives considered:

- experiment
- quasi-experiment
- observational study
- survey
- panel or longitudinal design
- text or computational study
- qualitative design
- mixed methods
- systematic review or meta-analysis

Explain why the chosen design best fits the question and the data reality.

### 3. Data Plan

Document:

- unit of analysis
- target population
- inclusion and exclusion rules
- publicly available data sources
- restricted or missing data sources
- collection or acquisition steps
- ethics, privacy, licensing, or IRB considerations

### 4. Measurement Plan

Define:

- outcomes
- main explanatory variables
- controls
- moderators or mediators, if relevant
- coding or annotation procedures
- derived variables and transformations

### 5. Analytical Schema

Be concrete. Include:

- descriptive statistics
- model family or analytical method
- identification logic
- estimation steps
- diagnostic checks
- robustness or sensitivity tests
- subgroup analyses
- validation approach
- interpretation rules

### 6. Reproducibility Plan

Specify:

- folder or project structure
- raw versus processed data separation
- scripts or notebooks required
- environment and dependency capture
- table and figure generation flow

## Minimum Standard

A good plan should be specific enough that another researcher could begin implementation without guessing the main analytical decisions.
