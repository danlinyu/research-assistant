# Journal Targeting And Manuscript Drafting

## Journal Selection

For each candidate journal, verify:

- aims and scope
- article types
- recent comparable papers
- methods commonly accepted
- word limits
- abstract structure rules
- reference style
- figure and table limits
- supplementary material rules
- data and code availability policies
- open-access options and fees if relevant

Do not assume these rules are stable. Check them at the time of use.

## Shortlisting Logic

Prefer journals where the study aligns on:

- topic
- method
- contribution level
- audience
- paper length

Reject journals that are a poor fit even if they are prestigious.

## Anti-Fabrication Rules

- Cite only papers you actually located and checked.
- Do not invent supporting citations for standard academic phrasing.
- If a claim needs support and no source has been verified, revise the sentence or mark it for later sourcing.
- Do not invent numerical results, effect sizes, p-values, sample sizes, or confidence intervals.
- If the analysis is pending, write a results-ready structure instead of fictional prose.

## IMRaD Drafting

### Introduction

Build from:

- problem importance
- what the literature already knows
- what it does not know
- the study's contribution
- the precise question or hypotheses

### Methods

Include:

- study design
- data sources
- sample construction
- measures
- analytical procedures
- robustness checks
- ethics and approvals when relevant

### Results

Report:

- sample description
- main analysis in the order specified by the analytical schema
- tables and figures tied to actual outputs
- robustness results

### Discussion

Interpret:

- what the findings mean
- how they compare with prior literature
- implications
- limitations
- future research

### Conclusion

Close briefly with the main contribution and implication.
